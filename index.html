VOLEUR 

Playing Cards Game For 2 to 5 Players

After a long day of misdeeds, it's time to unwind with your familiar accomplices. Brace yourself for a wild showdown of cards — only with a worthy hand will you steal victory.

Objective

Score as many points as possible over several rounds of trick-taking.

Each round, players compete with a hand of four cards, aiming to win the final trick.

Points are earned based on poker-style combinations in the player's hand.

Game Setup

  • Prepare a 52-cards deck ranked from 2 to Ace, and a way to keep score.

  • Shuffle the deck and deal six cards face-down to each player.

  • Players look at their hand, then discard two of their cards.

  • Afterwards, reveal the top card of the deck to determine the trump suit for the round.

Round Overview

A round consist of four tricks. For the first trick, players play one card simultaneously. The Player with the highest trump card wins the trick. If no trump cards are played, the single hightest-ranked card wins the trick, regardless of suit.

The winner of a trick leads the next one, opponents must follow suit. If they cannot, they must play a trump card; otherwise, they can play any card. If no trump card is played, the highest card in the lead suit wins the trick.

Tricks are not collected, players keep their played cards face-up in front of them. Play continues until the fourth trick is won, granting a scoring privilege. Then players gather their four respective cards for scoring.

Exception

In the first trick, if there is no winner, players start another trick simultaneously. For example, if heart is trump and players simultaneously play 9♠-9♦-Q♠-Q♣, it is a draw.

To earn points, players must have one of the following four-cards combinations in hand. If they have a basic combination, they score the points indicated. With a special combination, they steal the same number of points from any opponent.

Players score in clockwise order, starting with the player to the left of the winner. The winner scores last with a privilege: before determining a combination, he may replace one of his four cards with either the trump card or any card from an opponent's hand.

Scoring Situations

  • In any combination, the Ace can be used either as a One or following the King.

  • Scoring is limited to a single combination.

  • Without a valid combination, players doesn't score points.

  • Players cannot split the points they steal between opponents.

  • If a player is about to steal more points than the opponents have, he must instead score a basic combination.

Victory

At the end of the round, all players' cards — including the trump — are discarded. Each player then receives a fresh hand of six cards to begin the next round. The deck is reshuffled only when there are not enough cards left to deal.

Players continue to dispute rounds until one of them reaches 50 points. The player with the highest score at the end of that round is crowned Voleur, unrivaled Thief of Cards and Fortune.
